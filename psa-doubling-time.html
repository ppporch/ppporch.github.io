About PSA Doubling Time

PSA doubling time, often abbreviated PSADT, is an estimate of how long it would take a prostate-specific antigen (PSA) value to double if the current rate of change continued. It is one form of PSA kinetics, along with PSA velocity.

PSADT is usually calculated by fitting a line to the natural logarithm of PSA values over time. The slope of that line estimates the exponential rate of change. The doubling time is then calculated from the slope using the natural logarithm of 2.

How It Is Used

PSA doubling time is most often discussed in the setting of prostate cancer follow-up, especially when PSA is rising after treatment or in advanced disease monitoring. A shorter doubling time generally reflects a faster PSA rise. Interpretation depends heavily on clinical context, treatment history, assay variation, timing of values, and whether treatment changed during the measurement interval.

PSADT should not be treated as a standalone diagnosis or treatment decision. It is one data point that clinicians interpret alongside the patient history, pathology, imaging, treatment status, absolute PSA level, and applicable guidelines.

Practical Calculation Notes

A regression-based PSADT calculation is more stable when at least three PSA values are available. Many practical workflows emphasize recent values, commonly values from the last 12 months, so that older PSA behavior does not dilute the current trend. Values should be reviewed for accuracy before calculation, especially when they were captured by OCR from a lab table.

In the PSA Doubling Time app, OCR is used only to help capture possible PSA date and value pairs. The clinician reviews and confirms the values before calculation. The calculation itself is deterministic and performed on device.
